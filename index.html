Editing Workflow


1. Ingest, Review & Organization

  • Review all incoming footage thoroughly before cutting
  • Tag, label, and organize assets using bins and color-coding systems
  • Separate footage by scene, take quality, audio condition, and narrative purpose
  • Maintain clean project hierarchies to support long-form and revision-heavy projects

2. Editorial Process

  • Edit primarily by script or creative direction, working closely with a creative lead
  • Build assemblies and refined cuts with attention to pacing, continuity, and intent
  • Maintain versioned sequences to preserve iteration history and rollback safety

3. Graphics & Motion Integration

  • Integrate graphics and motion graphics where required
  • Coordinate visual elements with narrative beats rather than decorative overlays
  • Ensure graphics are modular and reusable across multiple deliverables

4. Subtitling & Accessibility

  • Produce accurate subtitles and captions
  • Use transcription and AI-assisted tools where appropriate, followed by manual review
  • Ensure subtitles align with platform standards and accessibility best practices

5. Deliverables & Export Strategy

  • Create multiple sequences per project for clean exports across formats (social, web, broadcast, archive)
  • Export with platform-specific settings and naming conventions
  • Perform post-export reviews to catch errors (sync, graphics, compression, typos)
  • Revise and re-export as needed to ensure delivery accuracy
