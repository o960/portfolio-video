Overview

I worked across multiple live broadcast roles including camera operation, event setup and teardown, and on-air graphics support for university sports productions. The aim was to deliver clear visuals and reliable information flow at game speed while maintaining Mustangs TV‚Äôs broadcast and brand standards.

  • Client/Org: Mustangs TV
  • Role: Crew Member
  • Tools: Canon AX 25, NewTek Broadcasting Software, NewTek Switcher + Main

What I did

  • Camera Operation: Operated action and ISO cameras for live sports broadcasts, maintaining framing, focus, and shot timing while following control-room cues.
  • Event / Field Crew: Supported setup/teardown and in-venue ops, executing camera plots, white balance, exposure checks, and coordination to ensure readiness.
  • Graphics Support: Assisted with on-air graphics coordination by communicating live play changes and supporting accurate score/clock/lower-third updates.

Process

  • Pre-Production: Reviewed assignments and shot sheets, participated in camera meetings, and completed equipment checks (cabling, intercoms, WB/exposure).
  • Live Production: Operated assigned cameras per director cues, communicating via PL and monitoring tally while adapting to live play changes.
  • Graphics & Coordination: Verified accuracy of score/clock/lower-thirds, coordinating timing and keying procedures with the TD.
  • Wrap & Strike: Locked/capped cameras, coiled cables correctly, returned gear to storage, and assisted teardown.

Results

  • Result 1: Delivered stable, well-framed live camera coverage that supported clean cuts and replays.
  • Result 2: Supported consistent brand presentation by adhering to established shot intent and graphics procedures.
  • Result 3: Helped maintain zero on-air errors by following control-room protocols and reacting quickly to cues.
